Features:

This coin was purchased to replace one with a lower grade, which happened to be the first ever coin of the CC Collection. I had purchased this coin to attempt to materially repair the harm done by the avoidable sale of the gold coin that had been passed to me by my maternal grandparents circa 2014. This lost gold coin was inherited by my maternal grandfather Paul from his parents. I chose this particular coin for several reasons, one of which is that Queen Elizabeth II was contemporary with my maternal grandparents.

Note:

On her gold sovereigns Queen Elizabeth II always faces right.

Following decimalisation, sovereigns in uncirculated (bullion) quality were issued starting in 1974, the first sovereign in proof quality being issued in 1979 except for the VIP Brilliant Proof in 1976, which was issued in extremely limited quantities. The last coin struck at the Royal Mint in Tower Hill, London in 1975 was a gold sovereign by Queen Elizabeth II. Production was finally moved to Llantrisant, Wales, in 1976.
